Description:  Chlamydiosis (Psittacosis) is a generalized infection affecting both birds and humans caused by Chlamydia psittaci.  The disease is transmitted by ingesting or inhaling chlamydiae contained in feces, nasal secretions or lacrimal secretions of infected birds.  Chlamydiae can survive for several months in dried feces.  If the infected bird is not stressed, then the disease may remain latent or only show mild symptoms.  Carriers of the disease can be clinically normal.  When affected birds become stressed they will excrete chlamydiae through feces and lacrimal/nasal secretion.  The incubation period of the disease will vary fro 5 to 98 days. 
There are four different forms of the disease.  The peracute form of the disease will cause sudden death or death within a short period of time without obvious signs.  The acute form of the disease shows all the signs and may result in death within one to two weeks.  The subacute form of the disease will show a variety of clinical signs that may disappear then reappear after a few days.  The chronic form of the disease is characterized by severe pectoral muscle wasting, leg and wing weakness, fluffing of the feathers, and lack of feathering around the eyes from previous bouts of conjunctivitis and blepharitis (inflamation of eyelids).
To properly isolate C. psittaci will require laboratory work on a euthanized infected bird.  Culturing of specimens from live birds is unreliable.
